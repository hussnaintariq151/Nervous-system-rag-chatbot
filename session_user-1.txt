User: what is  abducens nerve
Assistant: The abducens nerve, also known as cranial nerve VI, is one of the twelve cranial nerves in the human body. It is a motor nerve that primarily controls the lateral rectus muscle, which is responsible for moving the eye laterally (away from the midline of the body). This allows the eye to move horizontally outward. Dysfunction of the abducens nerve can result in issues with eye movement, such as double vision (diplopia) or inability to move the eye outward, a condition known as lateral rectus palsy.
